The Reality

This Isn't About Snoring. It's About Survival.

Each apnea event is like being held underwater — your airway collapses, oxygen stops, and your body fights to breathe.

Most people think sleep apnea means loud snoring and feeling tired. The truth is far more serious.

During an apnea event, your airway seals shut. No air gets in. No oxygen reaches your blood. Your oxygen saturation drops — sometimes to levels that would trigger alarms in any hospital. Your heart races. Your blood pressure spikes. Your brain sends a panic signal to wake you just enough to gasp for air.

Then you fall back asleep. And it happens again. 30, 50, sometimes 100+ times per hour.

Night after night, this cycle damages your cardiovascular system, strains your heart, impairs your cognition, and increases your risk of stroke, heart attack, diabetes, and depression. It's not just poor sleep — it's repeated asphyxiation, and your body keeps the score.

That's why getting CPAP therapy right isn't optional. It's not about comfort scores or compliance checkboxes. It's about keeping you breathing.

Expertise

Every Type of Sleep Apnea. Every Patient.

Different conditions demand different strategies. Cookie-cutter therapy doesn't work.

Obstructive (OSA)

Airway collapse during sleep. Requires careful pressure titration and mask selection to maintain patency without over-pressurizing.

Central (CSA)

Brain signal disruption — not obstruction. Often requires ASV or bilevel therapy with careful backup rate consideration.

Mixed Apnea

Both central and obstructive components. Demands nuanced therapy that addresses dual mechanisms without worsening either.

Positional

Events concentrated in specific sleep positions. Therapy adjustments and positional strategies can dramatically improve outcomes.
